📅 June 15, 2025 - Tuesday

Foundations of Professional Communication

PESE 300 is designed to strengthen our employability skills through hands-on practical methodologies. These encompass technical knowledge, practical hard skills, and the essential soft skills that distinguish exceptional candidates in competitive job markets.

Essential learnings from this foundational session:

  • Technical expertise must be complemented by effective presentation and communication abilities
  • Admitting knowledge gaps is acceptable; avoiding problem-solving discussions is not
  • Cultivating analytical thinking by questioning "why" embodies the engineering mindset
  • Building a professional portfolio (GitHub, projects) enables recruiters to evaluate your practical capabilities
  • Developing a growth mindset is crucial for continuous professional development

Practical Session: Self-Introduction

During the practical session where we introduced ourselves, I learned something powerful: never underestimate yourself

  • Say "Good Morning" like you mean it
  • Maintain strong eye contact
  • Stand or sit with the right posture
  • Speak clearly and confidently
  • Give value to your name
Key Takeaway: Your energy and attitude can truly put the spotlight on you
📅 July 22, 2025 - Tuesday

Introduction to SWOC Analysis

We delved into Unit 1: SWOC Analysis - a comprehensive framework for evaluating Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Challenges in personal and professional contexts.

Through an engaging rapid-fire activity where participants shared their strengths and weaknesses without hesitation, we discovered crucial insights:

  • Internal vs External Factors: Strengths and weaknesses are inherent personal attributes
  • Understanding Context: External support (like family) isn't a strength itself, but can help develop internal strengths
  • Opportunity Recognition: External positives include educational institutions, internship programs, and emerging technology platforms
  • Challenge Identification: External obstacles encompass rapidly evolving technology landscapes, environmental concerns, and economic fluctuations
📅 July 29, 2025 - Tuesday

SWOC Analysis: Jhumli Taliya Student Case Study

Our focus group conducted an in-depth analysis of the SWOC framework for a student from Jhumli Taliya aspiring to pursue B.Tech in AI/ML, exploring the unique challenges and opportunities faced by rural students in technology education:

Strengths

  • Curiosity & Willingness to Learn: Village students have hunger to prove themselves - a superpower in tech
  • Strong Basics: Traditional setups often build solid math foundations - key for AI/ML
  • Adaptability: Used to learning with limited resources, flexible and resilient

Weaknesses

  • Lack of Quality Resources: Fast internet, laptops, coding mentors not easily available
  • Limited Awareness: Many don't know what AI/ML is or career paths
  • English & Communication: Language becomes a barrier since most content is in English

Opportunities

  • Free Online Resources: Coursera, YouTube, Kaggle, ChatGPT
  • Government Scholarships: NSP, AICTE scholarships support rural students
  • Remote Work Culture: Work for top AI companies while living in your village

Challenges

  • Financial Struggles: Courses, devices, travel for interviews cost money
  • Traditional Career Pressure: Family might push for B.Ed, govt job, or farming
  • Risk Perception: AI/ML still feels new and risky to families
📅 August 5, 2025 - Tuesday

Case Study: SmartBin - College Tech Innovation

In-class assessment where our focus group analyzed the SmartBin project - an innovative smart dustbin system developed by second-year B.Tech students using IoT sensors, Wi-Fi connectivity, and mobile app notifications for efficient waste management.

Project Impact & Results:

  • Achieved 80% sensor accuracy with minor connectivity issues in low Wi-Fi coverage areas
  • Successfully reduced overflowing bins by 60%, significantly improving campus hygiene standards
  • Cost-effective solution at ₹3,000 per SmartBin, attracting interest from local NGOs for community scaling
  • Demonstrated practical application of IoT concepts learned in coursework

Discovering My Superpower

We explored the concept of superpowers beyond extraordinary abilities or material wealth. Superpowers can be our personal qualities and strengths.

🌟 My Superpower: Not giving up when it matters the most

When challenges seem insurmountable and others might quit, I find the strength to push through. This persistence has helped me tackle complex coding problems, complete difficult projects under tight deadlines, and support teammates during critical moments. It's not just about stubbornness - it's about having the mental resilience to find alternative solutions when the obvious path doesn't work. This quality has shaped my approach to learning, problem-solving, and personal growth throughout my engineering journey.
